Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley - Frankenstein

Welcome to the Mini Digital Edition of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein

This mini digital edition was made as part of a project for Text as Data II, taught at the University of Antwerp.
We have attempted to provide a digital version of the handwritten manuscript by encoding edits, deletions and additions made by both Mary Shelley and her husband, Percy Shelley.

ABOUT MARY W. SHELLEY
Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley was an English novelist. Shelley was born on August 30th, 1797 in London. At the age of sixteen, she eloped with the Romantic poet and philosopher, Percy Shelley. It was three years after this, during a rainy summer spent on Lake Geneva, that Shelley penned her renowned masterpiece, Frankenstein.
ABOUT THE MANUSCRIPT
This manuscript is written over a span of two notebooks consisting of a total of 300 pages and has been stored at Oxford's esteemed Bodleian Library for nearly two decades. The manuscript contains all edits, revisions and notes made by Mary and also furhter edits that were made by her husband, Percy B. Shelley.
